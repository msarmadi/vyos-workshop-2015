          VyOS guys are awesome, just saying.

          IPv6 1-1 NAT is something I think a fair number of small office folks will want. Anything less than small office connection (home use basically) is generally accepting one small allocation whole from the provider/ISP as is with no portability and living with it. Big enterprise can get their own portable allocations easily enough. It's the poor SMB's stuck in the middle that need a stable IPv6 address space inside for rule configuration, but don't get portable allocations from their providers who end up hurting. 1-1 NAT at least can preserve a stable space inside that can map to changes outside.

          While getting a HE.net allocation is portable as a workaround, it won't necessarily be native.

          VMware still has that annoyance of not preserving NIC order if not using static MAC address, but I doubt there is much that can be done about that aside from serious udev rule futzing.
